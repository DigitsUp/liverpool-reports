Liverpool Los Angeles · Q1 2026 · Retention Review

The program held.
March showed what's coming.

Klaviyo delivered $1.34M — 55% of total Shopify revenue — in a quarter where orders fell 25% and new customer acquisition dropped 27%. Flows finished effectively flat (+0.8% YoY) against that backdrop. SMS crossed $461K (+63% YoY) and is now 34% of all attributed revenue. January and February absorbed the contraction. March flipped: flows up 25.1% YoY, strongest 30-day cohort retention (10.73%) in eight months. Q2 is loaded.

Q1 Assessment
What Worked
SMS emerged as a real second channel — $461K, +63% YoY, 34% of attributed revenue. SMS flows alone delivered $373K, up 73.5%.
Flows held the line — $816K flat YoY against a business down 25% in orders. The backbone didn't move.
March inflection — Flow revenue up 25.1% YoY. The best month of the quarter and proof the program is accelerating.
Inventory flows broke out — Browse Abandonment +178%, Price Drop +381%, Back In Stock +152%. Three clean wins.
AOV quality held — Site-wide AOV up 5% YoY. Campaign email AOV up 7.9%. Buyers spending more per transaction.
List health improving in April — For the first time this year, new subscribers (4,408) outpaced exclusions (3,172). Every exclusion category down 50%+.
Where We're Soft
Campaign conversion compressed — Recipients up 45.9%, revenue down 23.6%. More reach, less return. RPR down 45.8%.
Email flow efficiency softer — Welcome Series email down 14.2% YoY. Root cause is fewer new customers entering top of funnel (−27%), not flow quality.
Transactional flow down 57% — Wonderment send volume dropped sharply in Q1 2026. Trigger or configuration issue — not a flow quality problem.
List still contracting — Q1 net change −10,290 email subscribers. Acquisition trending up Jan→Mar but churn running ahead. SMS churn nearly halved (improving).
Top campaign revenue halved — #1 campaign in 2026 generated $15.9K vs $29.4K for the top 2025 campaign. Same demand environment compressing conversion.

§ 04 · Q2 Roadmap

Q2 is already loaded.

10 flows in motion. Four priorities that move the number.

  1. 01 Diagnose Welcome Series by source — Segment performance by acquisition channel before any rebuild. Rule out audience mix vs. flow quality.
  2. 02 Rebuild campaign reminder cadence — The 2025 multi-touch reminder stack produced $19–26K per send. Reintroduce in Q2.
  3. 03 Scale inventory-triggered flows — Browse +178%, Price Drop +381%, Back In Stock +152%. Clear growth engines — expand across products.
  4. 04 Push SMS toward 40%+ share — SMS is at 34% of attributed revenue and still growing. Realistic target: 40%+ by end of Q2.
